What is this dire delight flowering
July 12, 2018

The front door of our home met the street at eye level; Embracing each commuter mid-stride. 2334 Middle Street. When we arrived here it was nothing but wall and sand. After the uprising, our family along with roughly 20,000 others, began an arduous journey across the lands of Ozymandius.For days we marched through the grains of sand, the wind rustling our flesh and cotton. The sun soaked dormitory of utter relentless rays of sunlight burned our skin where it was bare, and boiled it where it was not. Until, one day, deep into the horizon, we spotted her waving in the desert heat. Anabassis. A temple of wealth and knowledge. She was a town shrouded in mystery due to her secluded locale. There were tales of the Tulpa: a free people born of slaves, and though still low on the rungs of society, had been given the glossy moniker of success. For, across the known world, the Tulpa were regarded without question, to be the for most authority on carpentry as architects in the highest regard.

The aristocracyy, too was well known, though they rarely travelled outside of the cities' walls, the literary and intellectual achievements were beyond a flash of a doubt, the finest works of fiction the world had ever seen. Their temples are said to have existed before the arrivals of man, standing on their own volition, the city of Anabassis grew there as an afterthought. Believing that whoever built these temples, now standing alone at the base of a wellspring, had abandoned these holy relics long ago; They started to build. Those with the means, but the the skill to build, paid those among them skilled in the ways of woodworking, and in brick building and in architectural planning to build these new homes. Thus the Tulpa and the Aristocrats were born; Two classes inside the cities walls.

The pace of our footsteps, though haggard, increased as we lay eyes on that wall so famous and full of mystery. The sand sunk beneath my heels a bit slow, with more repugnance than ever befre. As if testing my resolve to complete this wicked and tiring journey. Our swarm of 20,000 (though uncounted since our journey began) had certainly dwindled.There were a handful of funerals to honor those wo, for one reason or another, couldn't continue on. These were all ceremonial of course, it being too risky to stop in the daylight, nor could anon muster the strength to carry a fallen friend, family member or stranger. If you died anytime before dusk, your body was left behind, strewn among the wind and sun along the dunes.

Anabassis being of such high report, and with great confidence, the intelligence of the town met us at the feet of their great wall with immense joy. Let us feed you, clothe you, bring you water. "Where are your wounded?" that asked,"The eldest among you? Those who are poor in health. Let us tend to them first. And the children, bring them to the front so that we may help them too. Then we shall talk, and begin to know each other more dearly." Blessings.

Our hearts were softened, our eyes well up with tears of joy; at the prospect of rest, of peace and of life. Deep into the evening and into the light of the next morning they fed us, brought us warmth and water. Blessings.
